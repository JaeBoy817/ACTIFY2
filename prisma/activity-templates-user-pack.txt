TITLE: Bingo
CATEGORY: Group Game | Cognitive | Social
DIFFICULTY: Easy
SUPPLIES: Bingo cards (standard or theme), bingo chips/daubers, number caller (cards/app), prize bin (optional), wipes/sanitizer, pen & attendance sheet, microphone (optional)
SET UP STEPS:
1) Choose bingo type (standard, picture, theme, holiday).
2) Set tables/chairs for clear sightlines and easy mobility access.
3) Pass out cards + chips/daubers (or pre-place at seats).
4) Explain rules (how to win, how to call “Bingo,” prize policy).
5) Start calling numbers with a steady pace and repeat each call twice.
CHECKLIST:
☐ Sanitize tables, chips, and daubers
☐ Distribute bingo cards
☐ Distribute chips/daubers
☐ Review rules and winning patterns
☐ Do a “practice call” for new players
☐ Call numbers clearly and repeat
☐ Verify each win before awarding
☐ Record attendance and winners
☐ Clean up supplies and reset room
BED-BOUND ADAPTATION: Bring a lap tray/overbed table, use a single large-print card, offer hand-over-hand or verbal cueing to mark spaces, shorten rounds.
DEMENTIA ADAPTATION: Use Picture Bingo or 4-Corners Bingo, slower pace, fewer cards per person, frequent reassurance, celebrate near-wins to keep mood positive.
LOW-VISION ADAPTATION: Large-print/high-contrast cards, bright contrasting markers, call loudly and repeat, announce the full number (“B–12”), offer a magnifier or tactile chips.
1:1 MINI ADAPTATION: Mini-bingo with 1 card bedside; staff calls 10–20 numbers only; use “first row” or “4 corners” to finish quickly.

TITLE: “Around the World” Travel Talk
CATEGORY: Cognitive | Social | Reminiscence
DIFFICULTY: Easy
SUPPLIES: Travel photos (optional), simple country/state prompts, music snippets (optional)
SET UP STEPS:
1) Pick 5 destinations (states/countries).
2) Share one fun fact per destination.
3) Ask residents if they’ve been there or want to go.
4) Play a short music snippet related to destination.
5) Close with “where would you go today?”
CHECKLIST:
☐ Choose destinations and prompts
☐ Share fun facts clearly
☐ Encourage stories and memories
☐ Keep pacing comfortable
☐ Document engagement
BED-BOUND ADAPTATION: Bedside “travel interview” with 3 destinations.
DEMENTIA ADAPTATION: Keep to familiar places; accept imaginative answers.
LOW-VISION ADAPTATION: Spoken activity; describe images aloud.
1:1 MINI ADAPTATION: 5-minute bedside travel chat.
